Roofing workers' compensation is among the most challenging lines to place in Louisiana. High hazard, hurricane surge exposure, and frequent claim activity make many standard carriers reluctant to write roofing WC in the state. But competitive options do exist — if you know where to look.

## Why Roofing WC Is Expensive in Louisiana

**Class code 5551** — the NCCI code for roofing — carries one of the highest loss rates in the country. Falls, heat exposure, and the physical demands of roofing create a high-frequency, high-severity claims environment.

Louisiana adds an additional layer: hurricane season. When storms hit the Gulf Coast, roofing contractors surge their hiring to handle emergency repairs. Surge crews are often less experienced than core staff, and working on damaged structures increases hazard exposure significantly.

## What Louisiana Roofing Contractors Need in a WC Policy

1. **Correct class coding** — Steep-slope and low-slope roofing may be coded differently. Make sure your policy reflects all types of roofing your crews perform.

2. **Subcontractor management** — Many Louisiana roofers use subcontractor crews. Every sub must carry their own WC, or their payroll is charged to your policy at audit.

3. **Storm-surge payroll planning** — Build hurricane season hiring into your annual payroll estimate. Under-reporting surge payroll creates large audit adjustments.

4. **Claims management plan** — A return-to-work program and prompt injury reporting are essential for protecting your X-Mod in this high-frequency class.

## Getting Competitive Louisiana Roofing WC Rates

The standard market often declines or rates roofing WC at non-competitive levels. Specialty carriers and program markets exist specifically for roofing contractors, offering competitive rates based on:

- Your actual claims history vs. industry average (X-Mod)
- The percentage of steep-slope vs. flat/low-slope work
- Subcontractor vs. direct employee payroll split
- Documentation of safety programs and OSHA compliance

Working with a Louisiana WC specialist who has access to specialty roofing markets gives you a significant pricing advantage over standard market options.

## Hurricane Season Considerations for Louisiana Roofers

Louisiana law has specific rules around employees brought in from out of state for storm response work. Ensure your policy includes interstate coverage endorsements if you send crews to neighboring states or bring crews in from other states.

Additionally, FEMA-funded storm response work may have specific insurance requirements beyond WC. Review your project contracts carefully.

## Managing Your X-Mod as a Louisiana Roofer

Roofing has above-average claim frequency. Every claim — even small medical-only claims — affects your X-Mod calculation. Steps to manage it:

1. Report every injury within 24 hours, even if minor
2. Implement a return-to-work program with light-duty options
3. Conduct regular toolbox talks and document safety training
4. Review your X-Mod calculation annually and dispute errors

A well-managed X-Mod in roofing can save a Louisiana contractor tens of thousands in annual WC premium.
