# Competitor & Market Notes — dairy-insurance.com

## Competitive landscape (dairy farm insurance)
- **National ag/carrier direct writers** (e.g., broader farm bureaus, Nationwide
  Agribusiness, Westfield) — broad farm-owner packages, but often generic:
  undervalue registered stock, exclude/thin milk spoilage, exclude pollution,
  and misclassify dairy labor under a single farm code.
- **Independent farm agents** — vary widely; many place generic FP policies and
  don't address the dairy-specific gaps (livestock mortality valuation,
  equipment + spoilage, manure-runoff environmental).
- **Specialty ag / E&S markets** — write the hard risks (manure exposure, prior
  barn-fire/mortality losses, OSHA history). Our access here is a differentiator.

## Differentiators for dairy-insurance.com
1. **Livestock mortality valued correctly** — individual scheduling of
   registered/show/high-genetic stock, full + named-peril options. Most
   generic FP policies pay flat grade-cattle rates.
2. **Equipment breakdown WITH milk spoilage** — including off-premises
   utility-interruption coverage. Most property forms exclude spoilage.
3. **Dedicated dairy pollution/environmental** — sudden + gradual, with
   nutrient-management-plan documentation support. Standard GL excludes it.
4. **Properly class-coded dairy workers' comp** — per-role codes for milking,
   feeding, equipment, office; seasonal/H-2A payroll handled.
5. **E&S market access** for declined dairies.
6. One coordinated program (no gaps between policies), A-rated carriers,
   15-min quotes, 2-hr claims response, all 50 states.

## Positioning
Own the exact phrase "dairy farm insurance" and the long-tail dairy-specific
terms (dairy cattle mortality, milk spoilage insurance, manure runoff
insurance, dairy workers comp). Avoid competing head-on with broad-line
carriers on generic "farm insurance" — compete on **dairy-specific coverage
gaps**.
